**Headline:**
I Know What It's Like

**Body Paragraphs:**

**Paragraph 1: The Corporate Grind**
For years, I worked in supply chain and demand planning for consumer packaged goods companies. From the outside, I looked successful. I was following the standard American dream trajectory, making moves every couple years, advancing up the ladder toward director and VP positions. People would say I was doing the right thing for my family, being responsible, climbing the corporate ladder.

But beneath that success, I was frustrated. I was working for someone else, and it rarely felt like what I was doing made a real difference. Every year brought new changes, new shuffles, getting yanked in different directions without proper training or preparation. It was mentally exhausting trying to manage all of it.

**Paragraph 2: The Breaking Point**
Then came the mandate. A few years ago, my employer suddenly required everyone back in the office three days a week. Never mind that I'd been doing my job perfectly fine from home, hitting every target, getting better numbers every month. Never mind that I worked with people scattered across the country, so being in an office didn't even make sense.

They just wanted bodies in seats. Micromanagement disguised as "collaboration."

That's when it clicked: Not all jobs need to be done sitting in an office. Work can happen anywhere with the digital tools we have today. But most companies are still stuck in the old playbook, and it's costing their employees (especially parents) everything.

**Paragraph 3: The Discovery**
In early 2024, I started experimenting with AI to automate processes in my own business. At first, it was just trying to make my life easier. Then I built my first real automation for content creation: blogs, social media, newsletters, all connected and working together.

What used to take me multiple hours every single day suddenly took about an hour. One hour. For a full week's worth of content.

That was the moment. If I could save myself this much time, imagine what this could do for other parent entrepreneurs who were drowning in the same manual work.

**Paragraph 4: The Mission**
So I made a choice. I kept my corporate job (I've got a family to support, after all), but I started building this business on the side. Not to help Fortune 500 companies with unlimited budgets. Not to serve tech startups with teams of developers.

I help parent entrepreneurs. People running businesses from home while raising kids. People who feel guilty for working during family time and guilty for not working enough to grow their business. People who are stuck doing manual work while watching their competitors scale with automation.

Because I've been there. I'm still there some days. And I know exactly what it's like.
